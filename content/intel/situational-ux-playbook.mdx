Operators constantly jump between high-level orchestration and manual overrides. This memo lists the interface primitives that survived real missions: redundant affordances, mnemonic key chords, and visuals tuned for dim green displays.

Highlights include annotated layouts, audio cues that double as state machines, and ergonomics experiments that explain why some Fitts' Law assumptions fail when you're wearing flight gloves.
